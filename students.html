Student Activities

Hands-on mathematics that's creative, engaging, and fun!

Welcome Students!

Get ready for an exciting day of mathematical exploration! You'll be assigned to a colored team (Red, Gold, Green, Blue, Silver, or Purple), and each team will experience three different hands-on activities throughout the day.

Your team assignment will be given to you at registration. Check your team's schedule below to see which activities you'll experience!

Activity Descriptions

Coloring to Resolve Conflict

How many colors are needed to color the regions of a map so that no two adjacent regions have the same color? Explore how the relaxing task of coloring maps can resolve conflict in a surprising number of situations.

Discover connections between art, geography, and mathematics while solving real-world problems through graph coloring theory.

Led by Chris Black

Cut Your Way to the Stars

Explore the symmetry of simple and not so simple geometric shapes using scissors and paper folding. It is amazing what can be cut out of a sheet of paper in a single straight cut, but it requires creative 3D imagination.

Challenge your spatial reasoning and discover the mathematical beauty hidden in paper folding and cutting.

Led by Jim Miller

Draw the Line: Discovering Euler Circuits

Explore Euler Circuits through hands-on activities and use them to solve the KÃ¶nigsberg Bridge problem and other real-life problems.

Learn about one of the most famous problems in mathematics and see how graph theory applies to everything from GPS routing to social networks.

Led by Mark Oursland

More than just a Number: Encoding Personal Information

Explore how personal data is encoded into driver's license numbers in Illinois using the Soundex system. Apply this process to your own name and birthday, calculating your own Illinois driver's license number.

Discover how mathematics helps organize and secure personal information in everyday documents.

Led by Sahadeb Upretee

Ready, Set, Bet!

The game Ready, Set, Bet simulates betting on horse racing with some horses moving more frequently than others. The game uses basic probability to adjust the odds and balance the strengths of the horses in order to provide close races even when the dice favor some horses over others.

Learn how probability and statistics work in real-world gambling and game design.

Led by Aaron Montgomery

VR and the Space Station

Use virtual reality to explore the inside of the International Space Station or to accept a mission to go on a space walk outside of it.

Experience the connection between mathematics, physics, and space exploration in an immersive virtual environment.

Led by Dominic Klyve
