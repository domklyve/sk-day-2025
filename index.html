Welcome to the First CWU Sonia Kovalevsky Day!

A celebration of mathematics, creativity, and possibility. Join us for an inspiring day of hands-on activities, engaging presentations, and mathematical exploration.

Saturday, October 18, 2025

We are thrilled to host the inaugural Sonia Kovalevsky Day at Central Washington University! This special event brings together students, teachers, and coaches for a day of mathematical discovery and inspiration.

Event Highlights

Opening Talk by Dr. Jenny Quinn
Director of the Seattle Universal Math Museum
10:10 – 10:40am in SAMU 104

What to Expect

Throughout the day, participants will engage in:

  • Interactive Sessions - Hands-on mathematical activities designed to spark curiosity
  • Virtual Reality Experiences - Explore the International Space Station
  • Mathematical Games - From probability to graph theory
  • Professional Development - Teachers will explore current topics in mathematics education
  • Mathematical Scavenger Hunt - Discover math throughout the SAMU building
  • Career Panel - Learn about diverse mathematical careers from CWU faculty

Who Should Attend?

This event is designed for high school students interested in mathematics and their teachers/coaches. Whether you're passionate about math or just curious about exploring it in new ways, Sonia Kovalevsky Day offers something for everyone.

Lunch is provided for all participants! Join us in the 1st Floor Lobby from 12:30 – 1:45pm.

About the Event

Sonia Kovalevsky Days are held at universities across the United States to encourage young women to explore mathematics and careers in STEM fields. These events honor the legacy of Sonia Kovalevsky, a pioneering mathematician who overcame tremendous obstacles to become the first woman to earn a doctorate in mathematics and the first to hold a full professorship at a modern university.

Our event welcomes students of all genders who are interested in discovering the beauty, creativity, and relevance of mathematics in our world.
