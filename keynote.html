Keynote Speaker

Dr. Jenny Quinn

Dr. Jenny Quinn’s personal mission is to make mathematics accessible, appreciated, and humane. Actively engaging people with math in both classrooms and communities, she believes mathematics should be a lever that lifts people toward their goals, not a barrier that keeps them from reaching their dreams.

An award-winning teacher, author, and scholar, Jenny earned her Ph.D. in mathematics from the University of Wisconsin– Madison and has taught math for more than thirty years at the college level—primarily at Occidental College and the University of Washington Tacoma. She also served as President of the Mathematical Association of America in 2021–2022.

Now, as Executive Director of the Seattle Universal Math Museum (SUMM), Jenny leads an organization whose mission is to spark each and every person to love math. In 2026, SUMM will open an exhibit space within a beloved Seattle science institution and launch a public math lecture series, Math Out Loud. Until then, the museum continues to bring hands-on, joyful math experiences to the community—hosting nearly 400 events last year and reaching more than 19,000 people.

Just as Sonia Kovalevskaya once defied expectations to unlock new mathematical truths and open doors for others, Jenny believes that every person can be a mathematical detective—curious, creative, and courageous in solving life’s mysteries. Solving Mathematical Mysteries invites each of us to follow in Sonia’s footsteps: to look deeper, think boldly, and discover the beauty and humanity hidden within math.
