Keynote Speaker

Dr. Jenny Quinn - Making Mathematics Accessible, Appreciated, and Humane

Dr. Jenny Quinn

Executive Director, Seattle Universal Math Museum

Dr. Jenny Quinn's personal mission is to make mathematics accessible, appreciated, and humane. Actively engaging people with math in both classrooms and communities, she believes mathematics should be a lever that lifts people toward their goals, not a barrier that keeps them from reaching their dreams.

Opening Talk: Saturday, October 18
10:10 – 10:40am in SAMU 104
All participants welcome!

An Award-Winning Educator and Scholar

An award-winning teacher, author, and scholar, Jenny earned her Ph.D. in mathematics from the University of Wisconsin–Madison and has taught math for more than thirty years at the college level—primarily at Occidental College and the University of Washington Tacoma.

Leadership in Mathematics Education

Jenny served as President of the Mathematical Association of America in 2021–2022, where she worked to advance the mission of supporting mathematics educators and promoting the understanding and appreciation of mathematics.

Seattle Universal Math Museum

Now, as Executive Director of the Seattle Universal Math Museum (SUMM), Jenny leads an organization whose mission is to spark each and every person to love math.

Bringing Math to the Community

In 2026, SUMM will open an exhibit space within a beloved Seattle science institution and launch a public math lecture series, Math Out Loud. Until then, the museum continues to bring hands-on, joyful math experiences to the community—hosting nearly 400 events last year and reaching more than 19,000 people.

SUMM's Impact

  • Nearly 400 events hosted annually
  • More than 19,000 people reached through community engagement
  • Bringing hands-on, joyful math experiences to diverse audiences
  • Making mathematics accessible to everyone, everywhere

Philosophy and Vision

Everyone Can Be a Mathematical Detective

Just as Sonia Kovalevskaya once defied expectations to unlock new mathematical truths and open doors for others, Jenny believes that every person can be a mathematical detective—curious, creative, and courageous in solving life's mysteries.

Solving Mathematical Mysteries

Solving Mathematical Mysteries invites each of us to follow in Sonia's footsteps: to look deeper, think boldly, and discover the beauty and humanity hidden within math.

A Perfect Match for Sonia Kovalevsky Day

Dr. Quinn's commitment to making mathematics accessible and joyful aligns perfectly with the spirit of Sonia Kovalevsky Day. Like Sonia, who overcame tremendous barriers to pursue her passion for mathematics, Jenny works tirelessly to remove the barriers that keep people from discovering their own mathematical potential.

Key Themes in Dr. Quinn's Work

Mathematics is for Everyone

No one should be excluded from mathematics because of their background, gender, or prior experiences. Everyone has the capacity to think mathematically and solve problems creatively.

Math Should Be Joyful

Mathematics isn't just about getting the right answer—it's about exploration, discovery, and the joy of understanding. When we approach math with curiosity and playfulness, it becomes accessible and engaging.

Hands-On Engagement

The best way to learn mathematics is by doing it. Through hands-on activities and real-world applications, people can develop a deep, intuitive understanding of mathematical concepts.

Building Community

Mathematics thrives when people work together, share ideas, and support each other's learning. Creating inclusive, welcoming spaces for mathematical exploration is essential.

What to Expect from the Opening Talk

Dr. Quinn's opening talk will set the tone for our day of mathematical exploration. Expect to be inspired, challenged, and energized as she shares:

  • Her vision for making mathematics accessible and joyful for everyone
  • Connections between Sonia Kovalevsky's legacy and modern mathematics education
  • Insights from her work at the Seattle Universal Math Museum
  • Encouragement to see yourself as a mathematical detective
  • Practical ways to bring more creativity and joy into mathematical learning

Whether you're a student discovering mathematics for the first time or a teacher with decades of experience, Dr. Quinn's message will resonate with you and inspire you to see mathematics in a new light.
