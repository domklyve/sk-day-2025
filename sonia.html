Who was Sonia Kovalevsky?

Born in Russia in 1850, Sonia (Sofya) Kovalevsky pursued mathematics despite formal barriers that kept women from university study. She audited courses at Heidelberg, studied privately with Karl Weierstrass in Berlin, and earned a pioneering doctorate for work on partial differential equations related to Saturn’s rings.

After moving to Sweden, she became a professor at Stockholm University (first extraordinary, then ordinary professor) and authored plays and an autobiographical novel. Her legacy lives on in named features like the lunar crater Kovalevskaya and asteroid 1859 Kovalevskaya, and in Sonia Kovalevsky Days across the U.S.

She showed that great mathematics and great humanity belong together.

Highlights

  • First woman to earn the modern mathematics Ph.D.
  • Professor at Stockholm University
  • Research in PDEs and celestial mechanics
  • Honored by crater, asteroid, and even a satellite named “Sofya”
